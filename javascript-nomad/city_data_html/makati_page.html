Makati greenbelt and rockwell are nice enough to stay long-term. Especially greenbelt is clean and safe, the greenbelt mall and park is outstanding. Around greenbelt you have three decent sized parks available. Outside of this small bubble of cosiness its chaos dirt and sketchiness. Stay away from Poblacion, its just disgusting. Best bet for a clean and comfortable place to stay are hotels and serviced apartments. Avoid airbnb, its all trash. BGC is another alternative, tough I don't like it much there, its too crowded and too much traffic for me.

A spot that that you pick in Makati is of crucial importance. I've stayed one month in Gramercy Residences, Poblacion only to have a base to visit other Asian countries like China, Korea, Japan. It's very chaotic neighborhood, dirty, in the night there are prostitutes, sexpats and viagra sellers stopping you when you walk in the street. Some of the streets in the night were full of cockroaches and rats, definitely not a good view. Apart from this Makati is known to have cockroach problem in the apartments themselves - in my apartment I had to kill on average 1 per day. And it was still very nice building. The traffic in Manila is super high, and the streets are not very organized - that means travelling anywhere will take you a lot of time. One time going from Makati to Intramuros took me 1 hour 30 minutes. Internet in my apartment was 50 mb/s and I was connected by LAN Cable, however still I had problems with connection (even though the speed was ok it wasn't stable which resulted in dropping from some calls etc.) I don't recommend staying for longer time but if you need to stay in Makati then pick area around greenbelt as the streets are more organized, clean and the area is just more nice. Good thing about Makati is that it has also some nice night life and good rooftop bars. However the only place I wold recommend staying in Manila is BGC in Taguig. This is a very organized area, clean with nice restaurants, bars, streets, lots of greenery. It's not chaotic and dirty. And the apartment prices are similar to Makati for 1 month stay.

This means we take all trips logged on Nomads.com, check which country of origin the user is from, then divide that total visit count by the country of origin's population. If we didn't, it'd just show the big countries like US, Brazil and India always on top. Instead, this shows which nationalities worldwide proportionally visit more or less, which is more useful.
